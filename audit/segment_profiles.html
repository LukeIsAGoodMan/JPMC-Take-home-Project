Segment 0: Senior/Retired Householders

20.1% of population · 2.2% >$50K · 0.35x enrichment
  • Mean age 63, median 66
  • Mostly married (60%), 22% widowed
  • 66% female
  • High school education dominant (36%)
  • ~2 weeks/year worked; 91% not in universe for class of worker
  • 57% householders, 35% spouses

Marketing approach: Retirement income products (annuities, CDs), estate planning, Medicare supplements, health/insurance cross-sell. Channels: direct mail, phone, community events.

Segment 1: Youth & Dependents

33.1% of population · 0.05% >$50K · 0.01x enrichment
  • Mean age 11, median 10
  • 94% never married
  • 70% education = "Children"
  • 74% child under 18 in household
  • 57% with both parents present, 19% mother only
  • ~1 week/year worked; almost entirely non-workforce
  • 90% nonfiler for tax purposes
Marketing approach: Family-indirect via parents/guardians. Youth savings accounts, education products, custodial accounts. Long-horizon relationship building. Channels: family-targeted digital, in-app.
Caution: Very low direct income incidence. Not suitable for income-based targeting.

Segment 2: Mid-Career Working Householders

46.7% of population · 12.5% >$50K · 2.12x enrichment
  • Mean age 39, median 38
  • 62% married-civilian spouse present
  • 54% male
  • 33% high school, 20% some college, 18% bachelor's
  • ~49 weeks/year worked; 41% full-time schedules
  • 55% householders, 28% spouses
  • 69% private sector, 9% self-employed
  • 60% joint tax filers under 65
Marketing approach: Premium financial products (investment, retirement planning), wealth-management cross-sell, career-stage offers. Channels: digital, targeted email, direct advisor contact.
This is the primary high-value targeting segment. The classifier's probability score provides a continuous sub-targeting dimension within this group.
